DMET

The density matrix embedding theory (DMET) was proposed by Garnet Chan in 2012. DMET is one of the class of quantum embedding theory.

Selective references are:

  • Knizia_2012
  • Knizia_2013
  • Wouters_2016

Up to date, there are some studies on implementation of DMET on quantum computers (basically VQE), but only a few:

  • A Hybrid C/Q Approach for Large-Scale Studies of Quantum Systems with DMET: Rubin in 2016; UCC-VQE
  • Solving the Hubbard model using DMET and the VQE: Mineh & Montanaro in 2022; swap networks
  • A Problem Decomposition Approach: Yamazaki in 2018; estimate resources for DMET
  • Optimizing electronic structure simulations on a trapped-ion quantum computer using problem decomposition: Kawashima in 2021
  • Reduced density matrix sampling: Tilly in 2021; sampling reduced DMET; energy-weighted

The question is, how is the performance of DMET on quantum computer (quantum advantage)? Why not so many people try implementing DMET on QC?
